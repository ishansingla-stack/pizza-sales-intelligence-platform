## 2. Customer Segmentation Strategy

### Identified Segments

Our clustering analysis revealed distinct customer segments based on ordering patterns. Here are the actionable segments for targeted marketing:

#### Segment A: Premium High-Volume Customers
**Characteristics:**
- Order expensive specialty pizzas (Brie Carre: $23.65, Greek: $20.04)
- Lower order frequency but higher value per order
- Preference for gourmet ingredients (6-7 toppings)

**Business Strategy:**
- VIP loyalty tier with exclusive menu previews
- Premium combo bundles ($40-50 price point)
- Targeted email campaigns for seasonal specialty pizzas

**Size:** ~3-5% of customer base | **Value:** High margin, premium positioning

---

#### Segment B: Value-Conscious Families
**Characteristics:**
- Order simpler, affordable pizzas (Pepperoni: $12.47, Hawaiian: $13.32)
- High order frequency, multiple pizzas per order
- 2-3 toppings, classic flavors

**Business Strategy:**
- Family meal deals (2-3 pizzas + sides)
- Weekday specials targeting dinner rushes
- Kids meal promotions

**Size:** ~15-20% of customer base | **Value:** High volume, repeat customers

---

#### Segment C: Core Regular Customers
**Characteristics:**
- Balanced mix of pizzas (California Chicken: $17.45, Thai Chicken: $18.29)
- Moderate spending ($15-18 per pizza)
- Variety seekers (5-6 toppings)

**Business Strategy:**
- "Try Something New" monthly featured bundles
- Loyalty rewards program
- Seasonal rotation promotions

**Size:** ~35-40% of customer base | **Value:** Stable revenue base

---

#### Segment D: Specialty Enthusiasts
**Characteristics:**
- Adventurous flavor preferences (Southwest Chicken: $18.08, Mexicana: $18.02)
- Complex topping combinations (7-8 ingredients)
- Mid-to-high price tolerance

**Business Strategy:**
- Limited-time specialty pizza launches
- "Chef's Special" bundles with unique combinations
- Early access to new menu items

**Size:** ~10-15% of customer base | **Value:** Brand evangelists, social media influence
